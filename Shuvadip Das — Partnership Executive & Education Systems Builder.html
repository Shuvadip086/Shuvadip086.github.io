About

The operational layer that makes programs run reliably.

Education programs in the field are operationally complex. A program running across 70 schools involves hundreds of classroom sessions, dozens of teachers with varying training levels, LMS usage that needs to be tracked, government relationships that need to be maintained, and field data that needs to flow from school visits into decisions at the program level.

My approach is to treat operational complexity as a systems problem. Every workflow that relies on someone remembering to do something is a liability. Every data point that lives in a chat message instead of a structured record is a gap waiting to cause a problem.

At Cholpori, I mapped the full field operations workflow and built the infrastructure to collect, track, and act on that data systematically — so the program does not depend on manual follow-up to stay on track.

Systems Showcase

How I build in practice.

Three case studies from Cholpori — each showing a real operational problem, the system designed to solve it, and what changed as a result.

01
Field Data Collection System
Cholpori Technologies · 2023–Present · 70+ schools
Google FormsApps ScriptAutomation
The problem

Field data from school visits was being collected manually — written notes, verbal reports, informal messages — then entered into a form after the fact. With 70+ schools, the reporting lag was 3–4 days. Details got lost, entries were inconsistent, and there was no reliable way to know whether a visit had happened, what was observed, or whether anything had been followed up on.

The system

I restructured the entire collection workflow. Field facilitators now complete a standardised Google Form immediately after each school visit — structured fields for school identity, observation category, classroom status, teacher compliance, and follow-up flags. Responses feed automatically into a master Google Sheet, the single source of record for all field activity.

On top of the Sheet, I built a Google Apps Script dashboard that aggregates data into a live operational view: school-by-school visit status, observation summaries, compliance patterns, and reporting gaps. For same-day updates requiring immediate attention, facilitators send structured WhatsApp reports directly to me in a defined format — usable records, not noise.

What changed

The programme moved from fragmented, manually-compiled records to a structured, near real-time data flow. Approximately 140 school visits are logged per month. Reporting lag reduced from 3–4 days to same-day. Programme decisions are made from data, not impression.

02
Classroom Intervention Monitoring
Cholpori Technologies · 2023–Present · 210+ teachers
M&ELMS TrackingTeacher Training
The problem

A programme operating across 70+ schools cannot verify classroom-level practice through coordination alone. Without a structured observation system, the programme was operating on assumption — that what was planned in training was actually happening in the classroom.

The system

I designed and run a structured classroom observation workflow. Field facilitators complete observation records on each visit: whether the digital learning session is active, whether the teacher is following standard teaching practice, platform usage behaviour, and any classroom issues.

Teacher training is responsive — when observation data shows a pattern of teachers struggling with a specific part of the platform or pedagogy, that becomes the focus of the next training intervention. LMS usage data, FGD findings, and survey responses are triangulated to build a complete picture of programme health at school level.

What changed

The programme has real visibility into what is happening in classrooms. LMS active session rates in monitored schools are consistently above 80%. Teacher training is adaptive rather than generic, running on a quarterly refresh cycle driven by what the observation data shows.

03
Government & Institutional Partnership
Cholpori Technologies · 2023–Present · Government & private schools
Gov. LiaisonStakeholder MgmtM&E Reporting
The problem

A digital learning programme inside government schools does not run on product quality alone. It runs on institutional trust. School administrators and government officials need to see curriculum alignment, minimal operational disruption, and consistent communication from someone accountable. Without that, even a well-designed programme loses access.

The system

I manage the liaison function directly — maintaining regular communication with school principals and government officials, sharing evaluation findings in a form relevant to their context, and ensuring the programme's presence is seen as an asset. Concerns raised by administrators are tracked and responded to. Curriculum alignment with national standards is a non-negotiable operating principle, not an afterthought.

What changed

The programme has sustained access and cooperation across 70+ partner schools. Government stakeholders remain engaged with programme findings. The institutional layer is stable enough that the team can focus on delivery rather than constantly re-negotiating access.
